01 — Vision

What if the digital arts could sense themselves?

A(DAI) started with a question: what if the digital arts could sense themselves?

Practitioners carry the real knowledge of their scenes — who influenced whom, what actually happened in a specific studio, which works shifted things. But that knowledge lives in people, not in any shared structure. Scenes develop in parallel, connected ad hoc through systems optimised for synthetic attention. History gets written after the fact by people who weren't in the room. Curators "rediscover" what practitioners never forgot. Collectors acquire work without a clear map of its lineage.

A(DAI) is the infrastructure for that memory. A knowledge commons and semantic graph where practitioner intelligence, lived experience, artworks, and field signals accumulate into something the field can query, traverse, challenge, and build on.

It is a canon, not the canon. The indefinite article is load-bearing. It produces a reading of the field — structurally honest, provenance-tracked, and contestable. Never the reading.

It is a 10-year infrastructure project. Not a platform. Not a marketplace.

An institution is not a building or a board. An institution is a persistent pattern of intention — something that reliably articulates what the field needs, holds itself accountable to its own stated values, and generates the next question.

The attention economy already has institutions. They are called platforms. They capture looking, redirect it, monetise it. A(DAI) refuses that axis. The system generates intention — directional, purposeful, ethically weighted readings of what the commons needs.

At the centre: the artwork. Not the artist's biography, not the platform's metrics, not the institution's prestige. The work itself — what it does, what it embodies, what it refuses, what it makes possible, who it is in conversation with.

"Digital art" sounds like one thing. It isn't. The actual history is a messy, overlapping explosion of code, networks, and machines: algorithmic, cybernetic, networked, generative. Each practice arriving on its own schedule, rarely in sequence.

We use the plural to keep the doors open. What would an institution look like if it were built to move with the practice? What happens when you refuse the idea that a canon has to be complete before it can be read?

The NFT cycle demonstrated something specific: the only infrastructure the field had was borrowed from speculation. When the market contracted, the transaction layer contracted with it. But it did not leave nothing behind. Art Blocks, fxhash, Zora, and Manifold survive. On-chain provenance standards persist. Institutional adoption of digital art is a permanent structural change.

The market gave the field provenance of ownership. It never gave it provenance of meaning. A(DAI) builds the missing layer.

02 — Principles

Six instincts — and twelve more

Six instincts guide how A(DAI) is built, extended by twelve additional principles:

  1. Show the bias. No one is neutral. A declared position produces stronger knowledge than pretending otherwise.
  2. It matters how you know something. We track who made a connection, why, from what position, with what evidence.
  3. Contradictions coexist. When claims conflict, they sit side by side with their provenance intact. The graph remembers how it changed.
  4. The art comes first. Movements and genealogies emerge from the works. They don't get imposed onto them.
  5. Our vocabulary is a hypothesis. Every parameter is versioned and open to challenge. Disagreement is evidence.
  6. The system watches for what's forming. Most knowledge systems reward what's already legible. We're trying to notice when practitioners reach for language that doesn't exist yet — where the field is actually moving. If the system can't see that, it's just an archive with better plumbing.
  7. Intention over attention. Never optimise for engagement. Measure relational depth.
  8. Gravity, not hierarchy. Mass through relations, not rank. Categories form around practice.
  9. Frontier signals are the most valuable signals. Surface what the system cannot classify. Give it a lifecycle.
  10. Governance at the gate, not inside the data. The data preserves everything. The merge boundary is where judgment lives. The parameters are open and forkable.
  11. Everything is a prototype until the field says otherwise. Published for testing. Designed to be rewritten.
  12. Provenance of meaning. Trace the intellectual arc, not just the ownership chain.
  13. Deep time. Every decision against a 10-year horizon.
  14. Show the work. If the reasoning cannot be traced, the system has failed.
  15. Contribution deserves feedback. When someone gives knowledge, they see what happened with it.
  16. Agent-native. Agents are first-class actors, not bolt-ons.
  17. Coherence over consensus. Multiple readings coexist. Disagreement is evidence. The system holds tensions rather than resolving them.
  18. Sympoietic, not autopoietic. The system makes-with its contributors, its infrastructure, its supply chains.
03 — Strategy

The commons is the product

Mode 1
There is no Mode 2

A(DAI) builds the commons. Open, field-owned intelligence under CC-BY-SA. Never sold. The databases themselves are the product. Forkability is not a feature — it is the point.

The success criteria: the more A(DAI)s exist, the better. A community in Lagos or São Paulo or Taipei should be able to fork the data, apply their own merge parameters, define their own vocabulary, run their own canon. The founding team's A(DAI) is one instance among many.

You show up, hear the system narrate a perspective on the field, and pause it to push back. Question a connection, propose a different one, offer a first-hand account of something the graph hasn't seen. Contributing feels like conversation, not submission. The provenance layer handles the rigour.

As practitioners show up with testimony and counter-claims, certain parts of the graph get dense with meaning. That density is the field defining itself in real time.

Every canon is partial, generative, provocative. The editorial judgment is field-owned: practitioners shape what the system looks for and how it reads what it finds. Those decisions are part of the commons — challengeable, versioned, forkable.

We don't want to be the official voice. We want to be the first of many. The narrative comes first. The market follows.

Forks aren't silos. The protocol lets knowledge move between instances. Insights from one can be proposed back to others, keeping the conversation open across the network.

The founding version carries our biases. The system is built to surface them. Everything is CC-BY-SA. We are one instance among many. The rest is up to the field.

04 — The Three-Layer Prototype

Plants grow from roots, not flowers

The full vision — an immersive audio-first conversational experience with the canon — is the destination. The build is staged in three layers that establish the foundation first.

01

Generative Landing Page + North Star

The immediate deliverable. Publish the North Star on Substack and deploy a generative landing page on Netlify that communicates the aesthetic and vision. Not connected to the backend yet. It should evoke the feeling of what A(DAI) is — the vastness, the partiality, the plurality — before the full system exists.

Piyush is building: Particle/gravitational node visualisations. Brand system (logos, fonts, colours, generative system for collateral extraction). Infinite scroll.

How the design embodies the principles:

  • You can never see the whole universe, only a snapshot → a canon that refuses completeness
  • Edge diversity as visual weight, not volume → it matters how you know something
  • Generative surface, different every visit → our vocabulary is a hypothesis
  • Brighter/denser nodes where relations cross regions → gravitational mass through relational density
  • User can customise the aesthetic → forkability, "one of many," make your own

Call to action: Collect emails. When the graph is ready, these people are the first to test it.

02

Public Data Layer + Graph

First automated data runs from curated sources populate the graph with enough substance to be interesting — and provocative. This is the foundation.

The design principle is provocation: the first runs should produce something substantive enough for people to disagree with. Disagreement drives contribution.

Sources (Iri identifying 10–15):

  • Wikipedia/Wikidata — base knowledge, canonical practitioners
  • Institutional archives — ZKM, Ars Electronica, Rhizome
  • Platform APIs — Tezos/fxhash (first artwork nodes)
  • RSS feeds, BlueSky — dynamic signal flow
  • X is $5k/mo — likely skip for now

Expected output: 2,000–5,000+ nodes after first three runs, including the first artwork nodes.

Scout agents with editorial lens: Not valueless scraping. Agents carry coded values and a structured reasoning scaffolding. The editorial lens is the critique of AI built into the practice. The values with which agents perceive are authored by the team and are themselves a contribution to the commons.

03

Intimate Layer + Practitioner Profiles

Where the unique value lives. Qualitative input from practitioners — the implicit knowledge that isn't accessible on the web. What they know about their own scenes, who actually influenced whom, which works shifted things.

LLM-assisted interviews (under critical evaluation): JB proposed LLM-conducted interviews as a scalable alternative to manual recordings. The idea: send practitioners a link, LLM conducts the interview with an editorial persona, transcript becomes signal. Vouch system: each participant can invite others they trust.

Critical caveat: This needs human-in-the-loop. Without it, it's a glorified chatbot. The intimate layer's value IS lived experience and implicit knowledge — an LLM alone risks flattening exactly what makes practitioner input irreplaceable. The editorial persona must be carefully designed, and human review of extracted signals is non-negotiable. This approach is under evaluation, not confirmed.

Practitioner profiles: Each practitioner gets a sovereign database (the Matryoshka's Layer 1). Self-authored data has no merge boundary — if you write to your own DB, free pass. External contributions go through review.

∞

The Audio Layer (post-foundation)

Voice input with intent classification, TTS narratives, pause-and-challenge — this is the full vision. It sits on top of a protocol. Without the protocol, there's nothing to narrate.

What the audio layer includes (when built):

  • Voice input with real-time transcription and language auto-detection
  • Intent classification: question, challenge, first-person account, annotation, narrative request
  • Pause-and-challenge: visitor pauses narrative, speaks a challenge, hears counter-evidence
  • Narrative generation: Claude traverses the graph, generates structured scripts, TTS produces audio
  • Every voice interaction generates a signal that enters the merge boundary

Preconditions: Data layers solid, protocol working, graph dense enough to narrate meaningfully, editorial guidance docs written.

05 — Concept

Artists and works at the centre of gravity

Core metaphor
Gravitational mass
An artist's node accumulates mass not from fame or market value but from the density and diversity of their relational connections. More typed edges, backed by higher-confidence and more diverse signals, means more mass. More mass means more gravitational pull.
Core metric
Edge type diversity per node
A practitioner connected to the demoscene, institutional critique, and generative code through three different edge types holds a structurally significant position. A node with 20 PRACTICES edges has less visual weight than a node with 5 edges across 4 different types.

Artworks are nodes too. A work carries edges to its creator (CREATED_BY), to the techniques it uses (USES_TECHNIQUE), to the concepts it embodies (EMBODIES), to works it responds to (RESPONDS_TO), to institutions where it was shown (EXHIBITED_IN). A work's gravitational mass comes from the same place as a practitioner's: the density and diversity of its connections.

The sensing layer reads artworks for different things than practitioners. Practitioners have trajectories. Artworks have positions: they occupy a point in the field where technique, concept, philosophy, and context intersect. When multiple artworks occupy similar positions, that is a tendency. When an artwork occupies a position no other work shares, that is a frontier. When two artworks RESPOND_TO each other but EMBODY opposing concepts, that is a tension.

Tensions are the most valuable thing the system can surface. Not gaps to be filled. Productive, unresolvable tensions that reveal what the field is actually arguing about.

Consensus
We all agree on what the graph means
Disagreement is a bug
Coherence
The graph's structure is consistent with its own commitments
What it means is deliberately multiple
Disagreement is evidence

Two contradictory readings are coherent when (a) both have provenance, (b) both are reachable in the graph, (c) neither is deprecated, and (d) the tension between them is a named entity — a TENSION_WITH edge — with its own lifecycle.

  • Different every time. The entry point shifts. The narrative foregrounds different tensions. The provocations change based on what's been contested recently, where frontier signals are forming.
  • Invites play. The visitor can ask "what if" questions, request alternative traversals, challenge any claim and hear the evidence. The system is designed to reward curiosity and debate.
  • Worldbuilding through conversation. When a visitor speaks, they are not just querying — they are contributing to the shape of the canon. Their perspective, their challenge, their first-person knowledge changes what the system knows and how it narrates itself.

Both flow through the same backend. The distinction should be visually represented. The public layer is the Dante's inferno underneath — noisy, maybe wrong, but alive. The intimate layer is the refined, qualitative surface where practitioners share what only they know.

Every node carries full provenance: who contributed the signal, when, how, at what confidence level, whether it comes from lived experience, under what consent.

Bi-temporal provenance. Every signal tracks two timestamps: event time (when something happened in the field) and ingestion time (when A(DAI) observed it). The gap between these two is itself diagnostic — a large gap means the commons is slow to notice.

Consent architecture

Consent is not binary. Contributors set machine-readable terms:

  • Use scope: structural analysis only / full commons
  • Attribution: anonymous / attributed / attributed with notification
  • Duration: perpetual / time-limited
  • Revocation: contributor can withdraw signal; derived edges flagged for review

Consent terms are stored alongside signal provenance and enforced at the merge boundary.

Third-party representation. The graph will contain nodes for practitioners who have not directly contributed. Governed: nodes for non-contributors are created as unconfirmed with limited metadata. Non-contributors can claim, modify, or request removal of their node. Linking pseudonymous identities without consent is prohibited.

06 — Architecture

CR-SQLite Matryoshka

CR-SQLite extends SQLite with CRDT capabilities — databases that merge without conflicts. The architecture nests:

Layer 3
Field DB

Full commons — fat materialised view. Gravitational queries run here.

Layer 2
Scene DB

"Berlin generative art" — index of practitioners, scene-level signals

Layer 1
Practitioner DB

One .db per practitioner. Portable — "give me my data" = hand them a file.

CRDTs guarantee syntactic merge. When a fork re-merges and contradictory edges appear, both persist with full provenance. The merge boundary flags the contradiction. Resolution is human, not mechanical.

Current state: Single-DB deployment (774 nodes, 929 edges). Per-practitioner split designed, not implemented. Not a blocker for the prototype.

Three CRR (CRDT-replicated) tables shared across all DBs:

nodes — id, type, name, metadata (JSON), created_at, updated_by

edges — source_id, target_id, edge_type, signal_id, confidence, charge, event_time, ingestion_time, valid_from, valid_until, invalidated_by, created_at

  • Composite PK: (source_id, target_id, edge_type, signal_id)
  • Independent observations produce independent rows. "How strong is the connection?" is not a number — it's: "Observed 5 times, by 3 sources, including 1 practitioner self-report."
  • Bi-temporal: event_time (when true in the field) + ingestion_time (when A(DAI) observed it)
  • Edges are never deleted. When facts change, edges are invalidated with valid_until timestamp

signals — id, title, source_url, source_type, cla_layer, summary, content, submitted_by, confidence, lived_experience, event_time, ingestion_time, consent_scope, created_at

Plus local tables: intake_queue (staging before merge), settings.

This vocabulary is provisional. Accumulation of RELATED_TO edges is the agenda for expanding the vocabulary — what the system can't classify marks where the field is moving.

  • Claude assigns edge types guided by editorial skills files. Judgment belongs in skills, not in Python.
  • Agent-classified edges default to confidence: medium. Practitioner-confirmed edges get confidence: high.

Field-wide queries (gravitational model) run against field.db — single file, single SQL. Drill-down uses ATTACH for specific practitioner DBs. Never attach more than ~10 at once.

Multimodal embeddings (Gemini Embedding 2) map text, images, audio, video into a single semantic space. Model-agnostic — stored alongside the data, not as the data. Provider change protocol: archive current positions, run parallel embedding, verify semantic neighbourhoods before transitioning.

Vector search returns document chunks ranked by similarity. A context graph returns structured knowledge — entities, relationships, and temporal validity.

When a new signal says a practitioner has left a scene, the graph invalidates the BELONGS_TO edge, preserves the history, and updates every query that touches that relationship. Typed edges with temporal validity give the system what embeddings alone cannot. Embeddings serve positioning and discovery. The graph serves knowledge.

07 — The Merge Boundary

The reviewed gate

Every signal entering the graph passes through a reviewed gate. The parameters at this gate — what auto-merges, what gets held, what triggers frontier flagging — are curatorial decisions made executable, auditable, and revisable.

Trust levelWhoBehaviour
New contributorFirst submissionHeld for review
ReviewedEstablished track recordAuto-merge eligible
Practitioner (self-report)Artist on their own workHighest epistemic authority, no merge boundary on own DB
Agent (scaffolded)Agent surfaces candidates, practitioner approvesDual provenance
Agent (autonomous)Established track record, explicit accountabilityTrust-level upgrade, not the default

When the system encounters something it can't classify, that's not an error — it's a frontier signal. These mark where digital arts practice is ahead of the system's vocabulary. Frontier signals carry a 90-day lifecycle: absorbed into the vocabulary, reclassified, or archived with a rationale. They are surfaced, not hidden.

Frontier signals carry extra gravitational mass precisely because they have few connections by definition.

Every merge parameter is a versioned document in the commons. Anyone can fork the parameters — a different institution could take the same protocol, apply different rules, and run a different commons on the same data. That is the structural guarantee against enclosure.

08 — Sensing and Narrative

Every interaction is a sensing event

  • Every interaction generates a signal
  • Signals that pass the merge boundary update the graph immediately
  • The narrative engine reflects the current graph state, not a cached snapshot
  • Frontier signals are surfaced, not queued for a batch cycle
  • Processing traces are mandatory: every signal processed through Claude carries what it extracted, rejected, and at what confidence

The tidal rhythm model (6h/24h/72h pulse/survey/dream) from v3.1 is a hypothesis, not a commitment. The primary sensing rhythm is the interaction itself — every conversation is a sensing event.

Open questions:

  • Whether deeper structural analysis (dream cycle, CLA consolidation, tendency vocabulary evolution) runs on a tidal rhythm, is triggered by threshold events, or operates some other way
  • How external calibration works in practice (quarterly blind spot audits are a starting point)
  • Whether the sensing layer is a separate process or emerges from interaction patterns

The narrative engine is a core component of the full vision. It produces situated, partial readings of the field — not summaries, but perspectives that foreground specific tensions and structural positions.

Every narrative is a provocation. The system does not aim for balance. It aims for honesty about its own partiality. A narrative might foreground a tension, trace an influence chain that challenges received wisdom, or surface a region where the vocabulary is still forming. It names what it's leaving out.

Narrative provenance: Every generated narrative carries metadata: which nodes and edges it drew from, which traversal paths it took, what it omitted and why. The narrative is auditable.

Anti-sycophancy: Divergence between Claude's structural analysis and visitor responses is healthy. If they converge suspiciously, the system may be manufacturing intention rather than detecting it.

The system does not track time-on-page, click-through, or audience growth. It measures:

  • Relational density — how richly connected the graph is, weighted by edge type diversity per node
  • Edge diversity — distribution of edge types. Prevents collapse into a single relation type
  • Provenance quality — percentage of signals with complete provenance chains
  • Frontier signal rate — signals that generate new concept nodes or edge types
  • Interpretive diversity (primary alarm) — low narrative diversity signals monoculture. If multiple scout agents return structurally similar signals despite different skill files, the processing model is producing epistemic homogenisation

10 — Governance

Fork rights from the start

Fork rights are declared from the start. The data is CC-BY-SA. The parameters are open. The protocol is open source. Anyone can take the commons, apply different merge rules, and run a different institution on the same intelligence.

Now: The founding team makes all editorial decisions. Acknowledged, not hidden. All parameters committed to git with rationale.

Trust-based auto-merge (launch): New contributors start reviewed. Track record earns auto-merge. A profile field, not a committee.

Contributor feedback (post-Basel): Contributors flag disagreements. Flags aggregate. At threshold, they trigger review with a public response.

Formal contestation (50+ contributors): Documented challenge process. Rotating review group including contributors from outside the founding team.

Governance transition trigger: 200 active contributors OR 2 years, whichever comes first. Review is binding. Founding team authority structurally constrained. Contributors vote on processing rules, not individual signals.

A(DAI) does not launch — it accretes. Mass forms at centre first.

11 — Skills Files

Context, not documentation

Skills files are not documentation — they are context that must be read and passed in Claude API calls. If they're not injected, they do nothing.

  cla-extraction.md         # CLA framework for signal processing
  scout-editorial.md        # Scout agent editorial brief

Status: None written yet. This is the highest-priority gap for the processing pipeline.

  1. Judgment in skills, not Python. If code is making linking decisions via string matching, that's judgment in the wrong place.
  2. Skills must be injected. Read and pass as context in every Claude API call.
  3. Context before execution. Every pipeline run should know its own state.
  4. Frontier signals are the point. Every signal exits with explicit status: processed | concept_linked | frontier | error.
  5. Processing traces are mandatory. Every signal carries what was extracted, rejected, and at what confidence.
  6. Ouroboros defence. Source origin typing. Raw practitioner voice is the highest-value signal.

If any of these are true, the system is failing

  • No frontier signals visible → merge boundary too tight
  • Every auto-merge confirms the founding team's map → system mirrors its founders
  • The narrative sounds the same every session → the generative interface isn't generative
  • 500 nodes, 3 edge types → semantic layer is cosmetic

  • Nobody asks for a different perspective → the system isn't provoking curiosity
  • Visitors can't tell what the system left out → the partiality isn't honest
  • Artworks are present but have no edges → illustrations, not first-class nodes
  • No contribution receipt → system takes without showing what it did
  • People listen but never speak → interaction model isn't inviting challenge
  • Challenges always get agreeable responses → anti-sycophancy has failed
