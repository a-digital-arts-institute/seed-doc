01

Vision

An institution is not a building or a board. An institution is a persistent pattern of intention.

A(DAI) is a digital arts institute. The indefinite article is load-bearing. The system produces a reading of the field — structurally honest, provenance-tracked, and contestable. Never the reading.

It is a 10-year infrastructure project. Not a platform. Not a marketplace. A knowledge commons: a semantic graph where practitioner intelligence, lived experience, and field signals accumulate into something the field can query, traverse, and build on.

The market gave the field provenance of ownership. It never gave it provenance of meaning. A(DAI) builds the missing layer.

Attention
"What's interesting?" Scans for novelty. Gravitates toward visibility. Reinforces concentration. Output: a feed, a ranking.
vs
Intention
"What does the commons need?" Scans for gaps. Gravitates toward what is absent. Redistributes. Output: a prompt, a tension, a structural diagnosis.
02

Strategy

Two modes, one commons. The advisory layer is only worth something because the commons beneath it is real.

Mode 1 is the commons. Open, field-owned intelligence under CC-BY-SA. It is never sold. It is the work.

Mode 2 is advisory services for capital actors — collectors, curators, institutions — built on top of Mode 1 without replacing it. Designed around specific decision-support: institutional acquisition context, conservation triage, exhibition programming.

Scaffolding, not delegation. Practitioner agents surface candidates. The practitioner reviews, judges, submits. The agent presents — it does not decide. Autonomous submission is a trust-level upgrade, not the starting configuration.

03

Concept

Artists and works at the centre of gravity. Tensions are the most valuable thing the system can surface.

The core metaphor is gravitational. A node accumulates mass from the density and diversity of its relational connections — not from fame or market value. Concept and scene nodes orient around the practitioner and artwork, not the reverse.

Coherence without consensus. Two contradictory readings are coherent when both have provenance, both are reachable, neither is deprecated, and the tension between them is a named entity. Disagreement is evidence.

Consent architecture. Machine-readable terms: use scope, attribution, duration, revocation. Third-party representation governed: unconfirmed nodes, self/other-contributed data distinction, pseudonym protection.

04

Governance

Architectural, not bureaucratic. Governance at the gate, not inside the data.
Now
Founding team makes all editorial decisions. Acknowledged, not hidden. All parameters committed to git with rationale.
Launch
Trust-based auto-merge. New contributors start reviewed, build track record, earn auto-merge. A profile field, not a committee.
Post-Basel
Contributor feedback. Flags aggregate. At threshold, trigger review with public response. Dissent logged even when the decision holds.
50+ contributors
Formal contestation. Documented challenge process. Rotating review group. Outcomes as precedent.
200+ contributors
Cooperative governance. Founding team authority structurally constrained. Contributors vote on processing rules, not individual signals. Fork right operative from day one.

The Rooms

Four rooms built for Basel. Two more declared as post-Basel direction. Each reads from and writes to the same graph.
01
Sense
Slow · High-density · Breathing
Artworks and practitioners in relational 3D space. Positions pre-computed from graph structure. Conversation fragments surface over the artwork layer. The visitor's first reaction: "I didn't know those two practices were connected."
Generates: ambient signal, presence as data
02
Query
Sharp · High-contrast · Tension
10–15 provocations pre-generated from real graph tensions. The graph asks, the visitor answers, the answer enters the graph. Your response does not resolve the tension. It adds mass to it.
Generates: tension edges, disagreement as mass
03
Contribute
Warm · Invitational · Structured
Encounter a generative reading of a subgraph. See a gap the graph has identified. Add an artwork, a practitioner, a reaction. The receipt shows what your contribution connected to.
Generates: new nodes, language for what resists classification
04
Connect
Open · Minimal · Emergent
When two practitioners discover shared conceptual territory through the graph, they open a thread — a named tension anchored to specific nodes. Lightweight identity: name, practice, 2–3 works.
Generates: collaboration edges, emergent social infrastructure
05
Experiment
Sparse · Frontier · Agent-native
The graph's sparse region made habitable. Canvas at a real coordinate in embedding space where signal density is lowest. Where practitioner agents live and scout.
Generates: new nodes from practice
06
Speculate
Layered · Typographic · Dense
The graph's narrative layer made editable. Manifesto assembled from tendency vocabulary, not pre-written. Click a line and you are proposing a vocabulary shift.
Generates: vocabulary signal, frontier language

Architecture

CR-SQLite Matryoshka. Agent-native backend. Every signal carries its reasoning.

Data layer. CR-SQLite with CRDT capabilities. Practitioner DB → Scene DB → Field DB. Nested, portable, mergeable. "Give me my data" = hand them a file.

Processing trace. Every signal processed through Claude carries what it extracted, rejected, and at what confidence. The pipeline is auditable from day one.

Ouroboros defense. Source origin typing (human_primary through ai_generated). Cycle detection. Convergence testing. Raw practitioner voice is the highest-value signal.

The Sensing Loop

Tidal rhythms. Not continuous, not on-demand. Slowness is the critique.
6h
The Pulse
Lightweight scan. Metrics. Flag orphans, imbalances, stale nodes, low interpretive diversity. Output: intention gap report.
24h
The Survey
Topological analysis. Clusters, bridges, structural holes. Artwork clustering. Contribution patterns. Output: intention observations.
72h
The Dream
CLA consolidation. Tendency vocabulary. Counter-signals and contested diagnoses. Artwork tensions into field-level questions. Output: generative prompts.

External calibration. Quarterly blind spot audits from outside the founding team's network. Coverage gap quantification against festival submissions, platform data, institutional records. Processing model bias comparison: Claude's extractions vs. raw signal content.

Anticipatory sensing. Frontier signal clustering (proto-tendencies). Vocabulary strain detection (concepts forming that the vocabulary cannot name). Institutional leading indicators (open calls vs. graph frontiers).

Rapid-response interrupt. Category-level disruptions escalate from pulse to dream in hours. Manual at launch, automated post-Basel.

08

Prompting Layer

Dual-register: same observations drive backend analysis and surface as room provocations.
Embodiment Dialogue Frontier Tension Absence Myth Trajectory Accountability

Anti-sycophancy. Divergence between Claude's analysis and practitioner responses is healthy. If they converge suspiciously, the system may be manufacturing intention rather than detecting it.

Contestability. Every prompt names its assumptions and invites disagreement. If no prompt is ever contested, the prompts are too safe.

09

Participation

Extend, fork, narrate. At Basel, all modes are text-based.
Extend
Add a node, deepen a node, extend the vocabulary. Three levels of structural contribution through the merge boundary.
Fork
Take the graph. Run your own garden. Own sensing, own prompts, own dream output. Merge proposals back via CRDT diff.
Narrate
Text-based walk through the graph. The system and visitor co-produce a reading. The contribution is the conversation itself. Audio post-Basel.

Design Principles

01
Intention over attention. Never optimise for engagement.
02
Gravity, not hierarchy. Mass through relations, not rank.
03
Frontier signals are the most valuable signals. Surface what the system cannot classify.
04
Governance at the gate. The data preserves everything. The merge boundary is where judgment lives.
05
Everything is a prototype until the field says otherwise.
06
Provenance of meaning. Trace the intellectual arc, not just the ownership chain.
07
Deep time. Every decision against a 10-year horizon.
08
The rooms are two-way. Every interaction reads from and writes to the same graph.
09
Show the work. If the reasoning cannot be traced, the system has failed.
10
Contribution deserves feedback. When someone gives knowledge, they see what happened with it.
11
Build bridges, not parallel systems. Getty, Wikidata, C2PA, IPFS.
12
Listen first. The system's first intelligence is recorded conversation.
13
The art is present. The system holds works, not just words about works.
14
Agent-native. Agents are first-class actors, not bolt-ons.
15
Coherence over consensus. Multiple readings coexist. Disagreement is evidence.
16
Sympoietic, not autopoietic. The system makes-with, not makes-itself.
17
The critique is in the architecture. Tidal rhythms, absence detection, fork rights — these are the argument.
18
Scaffolding over delegation. The practitioner's sensing capacity is the primary intelligence source.
